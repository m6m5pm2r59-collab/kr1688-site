How to Source Pet Supplies from China: A Complete Guide for Wholesale Buyers

By KR1688 Team · 8 min read · Updated July 2026

China produces over 60% of the world's pet products. If you're a pet store owner, Amazon seller, or wholesale distributor, sourcing directly from Chinese factories can triple your margins. But the process isn't straightforward — especially if you don't speak Chinese or understand how China's domestic B2B marketplace works. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Why Source Pet Supplies from China?

The global pet care market is projected to reach $350 billion by 2027. Chinese factories supply products at 30-60% below Western wholesale prices. Here's what that means for your business:

  • Pet toys that retail for $15 in the US cost $1-3 on Chinese B2B platforms
  • Smart pet feeders retailing at $80 cost $15-25 from Chinese manufacturers
  • Pet carriers and crates with 60-70% margins are readily available for dropshipping
Key insight: The margin isn't in finding the cheapest supplier. It's in finding a supplier who consistently delivers quality. One bad shipment of 500 defective chew toys will wipe out the savings from 10 good orders.

The Sourcing Process: Step by Step

Step 1: Define Your Product Requirements

Before contacting any supplier, know exactly what you need: material, size, color, packaging, certifications (FDA, CE, RoHS for electronics), and target price point. Vague requests get vague quotes and bad products.

Step 2: Find Suppliers on 1688

Search in Chinese using the right keywords. "宠物用品 跨境 一件代发" means "pet supplies cross-border dropshipping." Filter by transaction volume, factory certifications, and review scores. A supplier with 5,000+ transactions and a 4.8+ rating is usually reliable.

Step 3: Request Samples

Never place a bulk order without seeing samples. Pay for 2-3 samples from your top candidates. Compare quality, packaging, and communication speed. Most 1688 suppliers will ship samples within China for free or a small fee.

Step 4: Negotiate and Order

Once you've chosen a supplier, negotiate: unit price, MOQ, production time, and payment terms. Most 1688 suppliers accept 30% deposit with 70% before shipment. If you're using a sourcing agent, they handle this negotiation and payment on your behalf.

Step 5: Quality Inspection

This is the step most first-time buyers skip — and regret. Photos sent by the supplier show their best samples. You need someone on the ground to open boxes, check quantities, test functionality, and verify packaging before anything leaves China. A professional inspection costs $100-200 and saves thousands in returns and reputation damage.

Step 6: Shipping and Customs

Options range from express courier (DHL/UPS/FedEx, 3-7 days) for small orders, to air freight (7-15 days), to sea freight (25-40 days) for bulk. Factor in customs duties: pet toys typically face 0-5% duty in the US, while electronics may face higher rates. Your sourcing agent or freight forwarder handles documentation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Skipping inspection — The #1 cause of bad imports. Always inspect before shipping.
  2. Choosing the cheapest quote — If a price is 50% below others, something is wrong. Usually materials or missing certifications.
  3. Ignoring certifications — Pet products sold in the EU need REACH compliance. US markets may require FDA registration for certain items.
  4. Not building relationships — The best suppliers give priority to long-term buyers. Consistent small orders beat one big order.
