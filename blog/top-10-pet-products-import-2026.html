How to Choose the Right Products for Your Market

Not every trending product will work for your specific market. Ask yourself:

  1. Does it solve a real problem? Smart feeders solve feeding anxiety. Puzzle toys solve boredom. Products solving genuine pain points have staying power beyond trends.
  2. What's the shipping profile? Pet strollers have high margins but high shipping costs. Silicone mats are cheap to ship but competitive. Balance margin against logistics.
  3. Are there compliance risks? Electronic products need FCC/CE certification. Food-contact items need FDA/LFGB. Factor these costs into your margin calculations.
  4. Can you differentiate? If 500 sellers are importing the same silicone mat, you need a differentiator: custom color, bundle with bowl, branded packaging.

Sourcing Quality at the Right Price

Chinese factories produce at every quality tier. The key is matching the right tier to your market:

  • Budget tier: Amazon Basics competitors. Focus on volume, not features.
  • Mid-tier: Petco/PetSmart quality. Most importers target here — good margin, broad market.
  • Premium tier: Boutique pet stores. Materials, design, and packaging must justify the price.
